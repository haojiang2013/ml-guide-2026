Beginner Guide

01

Understanding the Goal

Mobile Legends is a 5v5 MOBA where two teams compete to destroy each other's base (the Nexus tower). A match typically lasts 10–20 minutes. Three lanes — Gold Lane, Mid Lane, and Exp Lane — connect the bases, flanked by a jungle area full of camps to farm.

💡 Tip: Your main objective is always the enemy's base. Kills and farm are tools — not the goal itself.
02

Picking Your First Hero

Start with a Tank or Fighter — they're forgiving when you make mistakes. Layla (Marksman) and Tigreal (Tank) are excellent free picks for newcomers. Avoid complex heroes like Fanny or Lancelot until you understand the game flow.

💡 Tip: Play 20+ matches on a single hero before switching. Mastery beats variety in solo queue.
03

Last Hitting & Gold Farming

Gold comes primarily from minion kills (last hits) and jungle camps. Stay in lane and farm consistently in the early game. Don't chase kills across the map — a fed carry wins games, not a roaming player who misses 50 minions.

04

Buying the Right Items

Use pre-made build templates until you understand each item's function. The in-game recommendation system is decent for beginners. Learn which items counter physical vs magical damage — Athena's Shield for magic, Antique Cuirass for physical.

05

Communication & Pings

Use the ping system constantly. "On my way!" keeps teammates from making bad calls. Ping missing enemies so your team plays cautiously. Never flame your team — a positive mindset statistically wins more games.

💡 Tip: Double-tap the minimap to ping danger quickly. It takes half a second and can save a teammate's life.

01

The Three Lanes

Gold Lane (Bottom): Primary farming lane for Marksmen. Focus on last-hitting minions and scaling into late game.

Mid Lane: Mages and some Assassins control this lane. Mid has the fastest roaming access to both side lanes — always help when you can.

Exp Lane (Top): Fighters and Tanks earn experience here. Winning this lane puts pressure on the enemy's side objectives.

02

The Jungle & Key Camps

The jungle Assassin farms monster camps for gold and buffs. Key camps include:

  • Lord (8min+) — Grants a powerful lane-pushing siege buff to your team. Highest priority objective after 8 minutes.
  • Turtle (2min) — Gives your whole team a shield. Contest every turtle spawn.
  • Blue/Red Buff — Reduces cooldowns (Blue) or adds slow/burn to attacks (Red). Crucial for junglers.
03

Rotating & Roaming

After clearing your wave, don't just stand in lane. Rotate to help teammates, counter-jungle the enemy, or take an uncontested objective. Map presence wins games — a frozen lane is wasted gold.

💡 Tip: Always clear your minion wave BEFORE rotating. Never rotate on a full wave — you'll lose 200+ gold in seconds.
04

Vision & Map Awareness

Check the minimap every 5–10 seconds. If you can't see 3+ enemies, back off or play safe. Buy Observer Wards (free in some modes) to deny enemy vision around Lord and Turtle spawns. Vision wins objectives.

01

Rank System Overview

Ranks from lowest to highest: Warrior → Elite → Master → Grandmaster → Epic → Legend → Mythic → Mythical Glory. Each rank requires winning matches to earn stars. Losing costs a star. Don't play ranked on tilt.

02

Hero Pool Strategy

Master 2–3 heroes across 2 different roles. If someone takes your main, you have a backup. Don't queue ranked with a hero you've never played. Practice in Classic first.

💡 Tip: Small hero pool = more consistent performance = faster rank climbing. Pro players often main 2–3 heroes their entire career.
03

When to Take Fights

Only engage when you have a numbers advantage, key cooldowns available, or an objective to contest. Never fight 4v5. Never chase a low-HP enemy into their base. Take the tower instead.

04

Mental & Session Management

Play a maximum of 3 ranked games per session if you're losing. Take breaks between losses. Turn off chat if you're getting tilted. Your win rate improves when you play calm and focused.

💡 Tip: Queue during peak hours (evenings, weekends) for better matchmaking quality and more experienced teammates.
GANK
When a roaming player (usually jungler) ambushes an enemy in a lane to secure a kill.
FEED
Dying repeatedly to the same enemy, giving them free gold and level advantages.
CC (Crowd Control)
Any ability that stuns, slows, silences, or displaces enemies — crucial in teamfights.
POKE
Dealing chip damage from a safe distance to wear down enemies before engaging.
SNOWBALL
Gaining a lead early and using it to dominate the rest of the game.
SPLIT PUSH
Pushing a lane alone while your team pressures another part of the map.
KITE
Attacking while moving to stay out of enemy melee range — essential for Marksmen.
ROTATION
Moving between lanes to assist teammates or take objectives after clearing your own lane.
DIVE
Attacking enemies under their own tower, risking taking tower damage to secure kills.
RECALL / BASE
Returning to your base to restore HP/Mana and buy items. Don't recall when visible to enemies.
BURST
Dealing a massive amount of damage in a very short time, usually to instantly eliminate targets.
SUSTAIN
Lifesteal, regeneration, or healing effects that keep you alive in extended fights.
PEEL
Using CC or body-blocking to protect a carry from being killed by enemy assassins.
ADC / Carry
The primary damage-dealing Marksman who is "carried" early and scales to carry late.
ZONE
Threatening an area to prevent enemies from entering it, controlling space without dealing damage.
ENGAGE / DISENGAGE
Initiating a fight (engage) or retreating from one safely (disengage). Timing is everything.
